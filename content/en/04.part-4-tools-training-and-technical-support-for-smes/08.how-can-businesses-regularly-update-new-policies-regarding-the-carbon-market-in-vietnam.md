# How can businesses regularly update new policies regarding the carbon market in Vietnam?

To effectively navigate the evolving regulatory landscape of Vietnam's carbon market, businesses should adopt a proactive, multi-channel approach that combines monitoring official government channels with engaging in industry networks and expert communities.

The foundation of this strategy is the regular monitoring of official government portals, which serve as the primary sources for all new decrees, circulars, and guidance; key platforms include the main portal of the Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment, the specific website of its Department of Climate Change (DCC), and the future National Registry System. In addition to these primary sources, businesses should subscribe to updates from key organizations, such as the Vietnam Chamber of Commerce and Industry (VCCI) and the Vietnam Business Council for Sustainable Development (VBCSD), which disseminate valuable summaries and analyses, as well as publications from international organizations like GIZ and UNDP that provide broader context. Furthermore, active participation in industry events, including workshops, webinars, and policy dialogues like the Vietnam Carbon Market Forum (VCMF), offers invaluable opportunities to hear directly from policymakers and network with experts.

Finally, for more tailored insights, businesses can utilize specialized information services, such as the newsletters and legal updates from major law firms and consulting companies that provide in-depth analysis on how new policies will impact operations. By combining these channels, businesses can ensure they have the timely and accurate information needed to adapt their strategies and maintain compliance in this developing market.
